Algorithms in the Real World

May 8, 2026

There is a common chargin among competitive programmers: you spend all your time training for algorithms, maybe a bit of time preparing for Leetcode, and complaining that your day to day software engineering job has no algorithms what so ever. What if, your interview is done algorithmically, and your day to day work is also algorithm heavy?

Throughout my career, I had seen a few examples of that from my own experience. It's true that finding those examples are quite rare in the software engineering landscape, but I had learned how to discover these problems.

With that in mind, let's get started.

Scheduling and resource allocation problems.

In this problem, we are given some resources to be allocated to different tenants. For example, a cloud provider may need to allocate computing power to different users, or networking bandwidth among different services. In this particular example, we will consider the most general form: we are given a network of links, and The goal is to maximize the utilization of the resources given the constraints. Formally, we can model this as a linear programming problem: $$ \begin{align*} \max_{x} & \sum_{i} c_i x_i \\ \text{s.t.} & \sum_{i} a_{ij} x_i \leq b_j \quad \forall j \\ & x_i \geq 0 \quad \forall i \end{align*} $$
